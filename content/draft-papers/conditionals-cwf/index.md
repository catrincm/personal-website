# Summary. An optional shortened abstract.
summary: "We show that Bacon's conditional is in fact equivalent to a simple strict conditional in a converse well-founded frame."

abstract: "In 'A New Conditional for Naive Truth Theory', Andrew Bacon provides a fixed-point construction for a conditional in a self-referential framework. His proof makes use of the Banach fixed point theorem, but we show that its result is equivalent to a simple strict conditional in a converse well-founded frame. We give a general construction for finding fixed points based on converse well-founded structures. In fact, many fixed point accounts can be seen as instances of this general setting."


#summary: "We show that many accounts for conditionals in self-referential settings are ultimately based on converse well-founded structures."

#abstract: "Self-referential sentences have been responsible for much work in mathematical and philosophical logic. A challenge of interest is to obtain attractive theories with interesting conditionals. There are accounts by Brady, Bacon and Field et al which aim to do this by constructing
#naive theories of truth or properties and classes augmented by a conditional and providing a
#semantic construction to show the consistency of their theories. In this paper, we highlight the
#reason that their constructions work: that they are based on converse well-founded frames,
#and note that this can then be seen as a thread running from the seminal characterisation results about provability logic to work on modal predicates. We are thus making the fundamental
#methodological underpinnings of these constructions transparent and show the continuity and
#common theme with other work in the semantics of self-referential sentences."
